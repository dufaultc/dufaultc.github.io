Materials Engineering Machine Learning Challenge

I completed this project as a member of a team of four at Delthacks V, January 26-27 2019. I had recently become interested in Data Science and was looking to apply some of my new skills. The goal of the challenge was to develop a system for recognizing what fractions of an image of a cross-section of a material are of what type.
I solved the problem first using Jupyter Notebook. My group members read in the pixel data from the training images, which I placed into a Pandas dataframe. I then created features using the RGB data about the pixels in the surrounding area of each pixel. I conditioned the features with Label and One-Hot Encoding, before performing a train-test split and fitting the data with a Logistic Regression Model from the Scikit-Learn Library. After validating the accuracy of the model, my group transformed the concept proven in the Jupyter Notebook into an application using the PySimpleGUI library as well as Pickles and Pillow. We also attempted to improve the model further by creating and manipulating more features. In the end we were able to achieve an accuracy of ~98% in predicting the fractions of inputted images.
